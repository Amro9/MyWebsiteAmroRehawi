Language may be a rough compression format for reality

Maybe the analog world can be represented digitally without forcing everything through labels first.

I believe there is a way to represent the entire analog world digitally.

Right now, we are doing it the hard way. We take what we feel, perceive, and experience, compress it into our language, and then translate that again into computer language.

Instead of naming things, we could rather describe them through their actual properties and states.

Example:

Not “apple”, but:

  • 70% green
  • 30% red
  • freshly picked
  • good condition
  • slightly soft
  • sweet smell

The computer would not store the label “apple”, but a measurable state pattern.

Same with emotions such as “sadness”:

  • little movement
  • slow speech
  • low energy
  • social withdrawal
  • specific brain or body signals

Again, not the label “sadness”, but a measurable configuration of states.

So a system could eventually understand emotions, situations, or thoughts much more precisely than through language alone. We do not do this every time because there is usually no need for it. But it feels good that a better way exists.

Takeaway

  • Language may only be a rough compression format for reality.
  • Labels remove information.
  • Properties and patterns preserve more of the original structure.
  • Many things may be measurable in theory, even if we do not currently measure them.
  • Objects may simply be bundles of properties.
