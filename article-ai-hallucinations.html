AI hallucinates. Humans do too.

They said AI hallucinates, and you need to be careful with what you take from it.

Well, thanks — this is a skill I’ve been practicing my whole life with humans. Because humans hallucinate in very similar ways.

Here are some examples of human hallucination:

  1. Have you ever seen someone read a word incorrectly just because it starts like another word? Someone says “speech” instead of “space.” The mind auto-completes patterns to save energy. That is a form of hallucination.

  2. Look at a picture for one minute. Memorize the details. Close your eyes and recreate it in your mind. Is it 100% identical to reality? No. Your brain fills the missing parts. That filling-in process is hallucination to save energy.

  3. We Syrians from Damascus are famous for acting like we know everything. We were rarely taught to say “I don’t know.” Hallucination became our best friend.

    You ask a Damascene for directions from A to B? He confidently hallucinates half the route. Not because he wanted to lie, but because feeling like we know something is often enough for us to believe we actually know it.

So the issue is not that AI hallucinates. Humans do too.

It’s just about what you cite is your responsibility, no matter where you get the information from.
